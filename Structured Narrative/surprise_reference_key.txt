What the Narrative Surprise (Focus 3) means
The Narrative Surprise layer measures whether management's earnings-call commentary was MORE BULLISH, MORE BEARISH, or IN LINE with what the market (consensus) expected going into the call. It sits on top of Focus 1 (absolute narrative level) and Focus 2 (quarter-over-quarter change), and uses the quantitative spine for the "what the street expected" anchor.

This is not a stock forecast. The LLM compares the transcript to a point-in-time CONSENSUS CONTEXT block (pre-call consensus, reported actuals, earnings surprises) built from parquet/narrative_quant, plus optional Focus 1 level scores. Post-call estimate revisions appear in the consensus block for guidance validation only — they are not used as LLM input for the primary surprise score (PIT).

surprise_direction
  more_bullish_than_expected  management sounded/guided more positively than consensus implied.
  more_bearish_than_expected  management sounded more cautiously or negatively than consensus implied.
  in_line                     narrative broadly matched expectations.

surprise_magnitude (-2.0 to +2.0, one decimal)
Primary Focus 3 signal. Sign = direction vs expectations; magnitude = how large the narrative-vs-consensus gap is. Same scale interpretation as Focus 1/2 tenths.

Quant anchors (deterministic)
  quant_z           standardized results/revision surprise vs company history (dim_*_z from parquet/dimension_scores).
  agrees_with_quant True when surprise_magnitude and quant_z point the same way; False when they diverge; omitted when either is flat.
  narrative_quant_gap  surprise_magnitude minus quant_z (quant clamped to [-2,2]). Positive = management sounded more bullish than the quant surprise alone suggests.

How to read claim + quote evidence in the HTML report
  Open output/{TICKER}/reports/feature_panel_report.html, select a quarter filter (not All),
  expand a row with +, and scroll to Focus 3 — Surprise for narrative-vs-consensus claims and quotes.
  The same pattern applies under Focus 1 and Focus 2 for level and delta evidence.

Relationship to other layers
  Focus 1 answers: how did management sound?
  Focus 2 answers: what changed vs last quarter?
  Focus 3 answers: was that story better or worse than the street expected?
  The gap between Level (+1.8) and negative quant z with positive surprise magnitude is classic narrative surprise.

Evidence
Same verification cascade as Focus 1/2 (verbatim -> composite -> anchored -> paraphrased -> unverified). Evidence excerpts are from the current transcript only.

Run
  python "Structured Narrative/run_surprise_scoring.py"
  python "Structured Narrative/build_feature_panel.py" --ticker {TICKER}
Requires json/dimension_view.json, parquet/narrative_quant, and transcripts (TRANSCRIPT_PROVIDER=local for the pilot).
Outputs: parquet/dimension_surprise.parquet, workbooks/narrative_layers.xlsx (3 sheets), json/surprise_view.json, audit/{period}_surprise.json.
HTML: claim + quote evidence appears in reports/feature_panel_report.html when a quarter filter is active.
